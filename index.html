Understand your bill

What's actually on
your electricity bill?

Most people only look at the total. Here's what every line actually means — and where your savings hide.

Sample Electric Bill
Service: 123 Main St | July 2024
$187.42
Energy Charge (kWh) 1 $98.40
TDU Delivery Charge 2 $41.18
Base Charge / Customer Fee 3 $9.95
Transmission & Distribution 4 $22.10
Taxes & Fees $15.79
Total Due $187.42
💡
Nova customers save an average of $40/month on bills like this one — just by switching to a better rate.
1

Energy Charge — the part you can change

This is what your provider charges per kilowatt-hour (kWh). It's the biggest line and the one we target. A better plan means a lower rate here, directly reducing your bill.

2

TDU Delivery — fixed, not negotiable

The TDU (Transmission & Distribution Utility — like Oncor or CenterPoint) owns the wires to your home. This charge is set by regulators and is the same no matter who your provider is.

3

Base / Customer Fee — watch for this

A flat monthly fee just for having an account. Some plans have zero base charge. Others charge $9–$15. This alone can make a "low rate" plan more expensive overall.

4

Transmission & Distribution riders

State-mandated fees that fund the grid, renewable energy programs, and infrastructure. Same for all providers — no savings here, but worth knowing what you're paying for.

★

The Real "Effective Rate" — what matters most

Divide your total bill by your kWh usage. That's your true cost per kWh. Most people are shocked. Send us your bill and we'll show you exactly what you could be paying instead.
